Japan's tertiary activity dropped for the sixth month in a row in
November, data from the Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry showed
on Friday.

The tertiary activity index declined 0.7 percent month-on-month in
November, after a 1.6 percent increase in October.

Among the individual components, medical, [health][1] care and welfare,
wholesale trade, retail trade, information and communications, real
estate, and transport and postal activities decreased in November.

Meanwhile, finance and insurance, [business][2]-related services, living
and amusement-related services, goods rental and leasing, and
electricity, gas, heat supply and water increased.

On a yearly basis, the tertiary activity index declined 3.7 percent in
November, following a 1.4 percent fall in the previous month.
