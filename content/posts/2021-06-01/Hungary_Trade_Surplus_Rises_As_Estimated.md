Hungary's trade surplus increased in March, as initially estimated,
final data from the Hungarian Central Statistical Office showed on
Tuesday.

The trade surplus increased to EUR 1.013 billion in March from EUR 328
million in the same month last year, as estimated.

In February, the trade surplus was EUR 849 million.

Exports grew 22.7 percent year-on-year in March, following a 1.9 percent
increase in February, as initially estimated.

Imports increased 17.3 percent annually in March, following a 5.3
percent rise in the preceding month. In the initial estimate, imports
rose 15.3 percent.
