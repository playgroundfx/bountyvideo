The manufacturing sector in South Korea climbed into expansion in
October, the latest survey from IHS Markit showed on Monday with a
manufacturing PMI score of 51.2.

That's up from 49.8 in September and it moves above the boom-or-bust
line of 50 that separates expansion from contraction.

Individually, output rose at its fastest pace in more than seven years,
while new orders increased for the first time since January. Business
expectations rose to a nine-month high.

A further reduction in employment levels weighed on the overall
[health][1] of the South Korean manufacturing sector.
