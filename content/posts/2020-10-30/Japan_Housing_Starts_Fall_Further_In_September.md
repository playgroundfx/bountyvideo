Japan's housing starts continued to fall sharply in September, data from
the Ministry of Land, Infrastructure, Transport and Tourism showed on
Friday.

Housing starts decreased 9.9 percent year-on-year in September,
following a 9.1 percent fall in August. Economists had forecast an
annual decline of 8.6 percent.

Annualized housing starts decreased to 815,000 in September from 819,000
in the previous month.

Data also showed that construction orders received by the big 50
contractors fell 10.6 percent yearly in September, after a 28.5 percent
increase in August.
