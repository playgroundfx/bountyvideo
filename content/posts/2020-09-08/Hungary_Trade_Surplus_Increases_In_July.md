Hungary's trade surplus increased in July, amid a fall in both exports
and imports, data from the Hungarian Central Statistical Office showed
on Tuesday.

The trade surplus increased to EUR 262 million in July from EUR 78
million in the same month last year. Economists had expected a surplus
of EUR 235.0 million.

Exports fell 5.6 percent year-on-year in July, following a 3.2 percent
decrease in June.

Imports decreased 7.7 percent annually in July, following a 5.1 percent
fall in the preceding month.
