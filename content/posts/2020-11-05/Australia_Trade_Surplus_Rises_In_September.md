Australia's trade surplus increased in September as exports rose and
imports declined, data published by the Australian Bureau of Statistics
showed Thursday.

The trade surplus increased to A$5.63 billion in September from A$2.618
billion in the previous month.

Exports of goods and services increased 4.0 percent on monthly basis in
September to A$33.737 billion.

Exports of non-rural goods rose 1.0 percent and that of rural goods
declined 2.0 percent. Non-monetary gold exports were grew 72.0 percent.

Imports declined 6.0 percent to A$28.108 billion in September.
