Private sector credit in Australia was flat on month in April, the
Reserve Bank of Australia said on Friday - following the 1.1 percent
increase in March.

On a yearly basis, credit rose 3.6 percent - unchanged from the March
reading.

Housing credit was up 0.2 percent on month and 3.1 percent on year,
while personal credit dropped 3.0 percent on month and 9.3 percent on
year and [business][1] credit added 0.1 percent on month and 6.7 percent
on year.

Broad money gained 2.9 percent on month and 9.3 percent on year.
