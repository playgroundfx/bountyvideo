Thailand's exports decreased more than expected in October, data from
commerce ministry revealed on Monday.

Exports declined 6.71 percent on a yearly basis in October, bigger than
the 3.9 percent decrease seen in September. Economists had forecast an
annual fall of 6 percent.

At the same time, the decline in imports deepened to 14.32 percent from
9.1 percent a month ago. This was also larger than the 13.4 percent fall
economists' had forecast.

The trade surplus decreased to $2.05 billion from $2.23 billion in the
previous month. The expected level was $1.94 billion.
