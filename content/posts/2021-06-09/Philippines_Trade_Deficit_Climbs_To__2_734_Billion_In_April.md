The Philippines posted a merchandise trade deficit of $2.734 billion in
April, the National Statistics Office said on Wednesday.

That follows the $2.412 shortfall in March.

Imports skyrocketed 140.9 percent on year after rising 16.6 percent in
the previous month.

Exports plummeted 41.3 percent on year after jumping 31.6 percent a
month earlier.
