Romania's [economy][1] recovered as initially estimated in the third
quarter, final data from the National Institute of Statistics showed
Tuesday.

Gross domestic product grew 5.6 percent sequentially, reversing a sharp
12.2 percent decline in the second quarter. The rate came in line with
the preliminary estimate released on November 13.

On a yearly basis, GDP contracted 6 percent after easing 10.3 percent in
the previous quarter. The annual fall also matched flash estimate.

During January to September period, GDP was down 5.1 percent from the
same period last year.
