Malta's harmonized annual inflation increased in July, data from the
National Statistics Office showed on Wednesday.

The harmonized index of consumer prices, or HICP, rose 0.3 percent year-
on-year in July, following a 0.2 percent rise in June.

Education cost increased 19.7 percent in July and [health][1] cost
increased by 1.1 percent. Prices for recreation and culture gained 2.0
percent and those of food and non-alcoholic beverages rose 2.9 percent.

Compared to the previous month, the HICP rose 0.2 percent in July.
