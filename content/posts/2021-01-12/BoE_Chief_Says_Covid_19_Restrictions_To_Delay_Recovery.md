Bank of England Governor Andrew Bailey reportedly said the third
national lockdown to contain the spread of the [coronavirus][1] will
delay the economic recovery.

In an online speech to the Scottish Chambers of Commerce, he said the
second wave would 'delay, probably, the trajectory' of recovery.

Although the unemployment rate is officially at 4.9 percent, the rate is
likely to be around 6.5 percent, he noted.

Regarding negative interest rates, he reportedly said that there is
nothing to stop it at all. However there are a lot of issues with it.
