Hungary's trade surplus increased in August, amid a fall in both exports
and imports, data from the Hungarian Central Statistical Office showed
on Friday.

The trade surplus increased to EUR 251 million in August from EUR 15
million in the same month last year. Economists had expected a surplus
of EUR 140 million.

Exports fell 2.5 percent year-on-year in August, after a 4.3 percent
decrease in July.

Imports decreased 5.4 percent annually in August, following a 5.8
percent fall in the preceding month.
