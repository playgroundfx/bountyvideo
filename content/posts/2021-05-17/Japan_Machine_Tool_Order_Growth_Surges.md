Japan's machine tool orders rose for a fifth straight month in April and
the pace of growth accelerated sharply, figures from the Japan Machine
Tool Builders' Association showed Monday.

Machine tool orders grew 120.8 percent year-on-year following a 65.1
percent rise in March.

Domestic demand surged 170.3 percent and foreign orders grew 251.3
percent.

Compared to the previous month, orders decreased 3.1 percent in April
after a 21.1 percent increase in February.

Orders in the domestic market fell 11.1 percent, while those for the
foreign market grew 0.6 percent.
