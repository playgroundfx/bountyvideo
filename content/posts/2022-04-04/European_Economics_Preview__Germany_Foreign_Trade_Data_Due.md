Foreign trade from Germany and [investor](https://www.fintechee.com/tutorial-for-forex-trading/investor-mode/) confidence survey results from
eurozone are due on Monday, headlining a light day for the European
economic [news](https://www.letsplayfx.com/blog/forex-news-website/).

At 2.00 am ET, Destatis is slated to issue Germany's foreign trade data
for February. Exports are forecast to climb 1.5 percent on month,
reversing a 2.8 percent fall in January. Economists expect imports to
grow 1.4 percent, in contrast to the 4.2 percent decline a month ago.

At 3.00 am ET, Spain's unemployment data is due.

In the meantime, consumer and producer prices are due from Turkey.
Economists expect inflation to surge to 61.6 percent in March from 54.4
percent in February.

At 4.30 am ET, Eurozone Sentix [investor](https://www.fintechee.com/tutorial-for-forex-trading/investor-mode/) confidence survey results are
due. The sentiment index is forecast to fall to -9.2 in April from -7.0
in March.
