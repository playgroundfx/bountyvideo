The Philippines posted a merchandise trade deficit of $4.016 billion in
October, the statistics bureau said on Friday.

That follows the $3.995 billion shortfall in September.

Imports surged 25.1 percent on year, up from the upwardly revised 24.9
percent jump in the previous month (originally 24.8 percent).

Exports added an annual 2.0 percent, easing from the upwardly revised
6.4 percent increase a month earlier (originally 6.3 percent).
