New Zealand credit card spending grew in October, the Reserve Bank of
New Zealand said on Friday.

Credit card spending increased 8.4 percent monthly in October, after a
3.3 percent declined in September. Spending rose for the first time in
three months.

On a yearly basis, credit card spending fell to 5.6 percent in October,
following a 12.8 percent decrease in the previous month.

Domestic billing increased 9.3 percent monthly to NZ$3.328 billion and
overseas billings grew to NZ$305 million in October, data showed.
