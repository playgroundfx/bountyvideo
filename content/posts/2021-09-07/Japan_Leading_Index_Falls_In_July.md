Japan's leading index decreased in July after rising in the previous
month, preliminary data from the Cabinet Office showed on Tuesday.

The leading index, which measures the future economic activity, fell to
104.1 in July from 104.6 in June. In May, the index was 103.1.

The coincident index decreased to 94.5 in July from 94.6 in the previous
month. In July, the index was 92.5.

The lagging index declined to 93.8 in July from 94.0 in the prior month.
In July, the index was 93.0.
