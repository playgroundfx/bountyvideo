Singapore will on Tuesday release January numbers for consumer prices,
highlighting a modest day for Asia-Pacific economic activity.

In December, overall inflation was up 0.4 percent on month and flat on
year, while core CPI fell an annual 0.3 percent.

New Zealand will provide Q4 figures for retail sales; in the previous
three months, sales were up 28.0 percent on quarter and 8.3 percent on
year.

China will see January numbers for its house price index; in December,
prices were up 3.8 percent on year.

Finally, the [markets][1] in Japan are closed on Tuesday for the
emperor's birthday and will re-open on Wednesday.
