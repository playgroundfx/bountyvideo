Japan's leading index increased at a less than estimated pace in June,
data the Cabinet Office showed on Wednesday

The leading index, which measures the future economic activity, rose to
84.4 in June from 78.3 in May. In the initial estimate, the reading was
85.0.

The coincident index increased to 76.6 in June from 72.9 in the previous
month. According to the initial estimate, the reading was 76.4.

The lagging index rose to 93.4 in June versus 93.3 in the initial
estimate. In May, the reading was 92.5.
