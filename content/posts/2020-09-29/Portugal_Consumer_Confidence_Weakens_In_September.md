Portugal's consumer confidence weakened for the first time in three
months in September, while [business][1] confidence improved further,
survey data from Statistics Portugal showed on Tuesday.  
  
The consumer confidence indicator eased to -26.3 from -26 in August. The
index rebounded after hitting a low of -33.1 in June.  
  
The manufacturing confidence index rose for a third straight month in
September to -14.3 from -17.3 in the previous month.

Morale improved sharply in services, wholesale and retail trade, and
construction sectors.  
  
The overall economic sentiment indicator recovered strongly to -0.5 from
-1.3 in August.
