Australia will on Thursday release September figures for imports,
exports, trade balance and retail sales, highlighting a light day for
Asia-Pacific economic activity.

In August, imports were down 1.0 percent on month and exports were up
4.0 percent for a trade surplus of A$15.077 billion. Retail sales are
expected to rise 1.3 percent on month after sinking 1.7 percent in
August.

Japan will see October results for the services and composite indexes
from Jibun Bank; in September, their scores were 47.8 and 47.9,
respectively.

Thailand will see October figures for its consumer confidence index; in
September, the index score was 41.4.

Finally, the [markets][1] in Malaysia and Singapore are closed on
Thursday for Diwali and will re-open on Friday.
