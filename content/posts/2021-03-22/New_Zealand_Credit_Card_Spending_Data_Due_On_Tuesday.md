New Zealand will on Tuesday release February figures for credit card
spending, highlighting a light day for Asia-Pacific economic activity.
In January, card spending was down 10.6 percent on year.

Singapore will provide February numbers for consumer prices. In January,
overall inflation was flat on month and up 0.2 percent on year, while
core CPI fell an annual 0.2 percent.

Taiwan will see February figures for industrial production and retail
sales; in January, they were up 18.81 percent and 3.56 percent,
respectively.
