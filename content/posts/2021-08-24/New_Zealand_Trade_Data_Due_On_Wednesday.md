New Zealand will on Wednesday release July figures for imports, exports
and trade balance, highlighting a light day for Asia-Pacific economic
activity.

In June, imports were worth NZ$5.69 billion and exports were at NZ$5.95
billion for a trade surplus of NZ$261 million.

Australia will see Q2 figures for construction work done, with forecasts
suggesting an increase of 2.5 percent on quarter - up from 2.4 percent
in the three months prior.

Japan will see final June results for its leading and coincident
economic indexes; the previous readings were 102.6 and 92.1,
respectively.
