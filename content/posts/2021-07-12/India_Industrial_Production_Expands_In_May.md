India's industrial production grew notably in May, largely reflecting
low base effects, official data showed on Monday.  
  
Industrial output grew 29.3 percent on a yearly basis in May, after
surging 134.6 percent in April. Output was forecast to advance 32
percent.

Manufacturing output registered a double-digit growth of 34.5 percent
and mining output advanced 23.3 percent. At the same time, electricity
output was up 7.5 percent.

During April to May period, industrial output expanded sharply by 68.8
percent from the same period last year.
