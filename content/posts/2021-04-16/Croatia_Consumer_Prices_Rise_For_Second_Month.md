Croatia's consumer prices increased for the second straight month in
March, figures from the Croatian Bureau of Statistics showed on Friday.

The consumer price index rose 1.2 percent year-on-year in March,
following a 0.3 percent increase in February.

Prices for alcoholic beverages and tobacco increased 10.3 percent yearly
in March and those of transport grew 5.8 percent.

Prices for recreation and culture gained 1.7 percent. Prices for
[health][1] and miscellaneous goods and services rose by 1.2 percent,
each.

On a monthly basis, consumer prices rose 1.1 percent in March, following
a 0.3 percent growth in the prior month.
