New Zealand will on Friday release May numbers for imports, exports and
trade balance, highlighting a light day for Asia-Pacific economic
activity.

In April, imports were worth NZ$4.98 billion and exports were at NZ$5.37
billion for a trade surplus of NZ$388 million.

Japan will see June figures for Tokyo inflation; in May, overall CPI was
down 0.4 percent on year and core CPI was down an annual 0.2 percent.

Singapore will provide May figures for industrial production; in April,
output was up 1.0 percent on month and 2.1 percent on year.
