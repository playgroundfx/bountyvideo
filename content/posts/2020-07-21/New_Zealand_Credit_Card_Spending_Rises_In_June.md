New Zealand's credit card spending increased at a softer rate in June,
figures from the Reserve Bank of New Zealand showed on Tuesday.

Overall credit card spending rose 14.0 percent month-on-month in June,
following a 59.4 percent increase in May.

Domestic billing increased 14.5 percent monthly to NZ$3.473 billion and
overseas billings declined 2.9 percent to NZ$244 million, data showed.

On a yearly basis, credit card spending decreased 9.2 percent in June,
following a 20.6 percent decline in the previous month. This was the
fourth consecutive fall.
